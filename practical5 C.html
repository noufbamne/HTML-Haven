Joint Photographic Expert Group

JPEG Files are image that have been compressed to store a lot of information in small file. JPEG (Joint Photographic Experts Group) is a widely used image compression standard. It employs a lossy compression technique to reduce the file size of images while maintaining a visually acceptable level of quality. JPEG is commonly used for storing and sharing photographs on the internet and in various digital applications.

TCP/IP Protocol:
TCP/IP (Transmission Control Protocol/Internet Protocol) is a suite of communication protocols that form the backbone of the internet. TCP/IP defines how data is transmitted and received over networks, ensuring reliable and efficient communication between devices. TCP/IP is fundamental to the functioning of the internet and is used for a wide range of applications, including web browsing, email, and file transfer.. HTTP is a member of TCP/IP Protocol.
